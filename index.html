I. The Absurd Mandate

Welcome, delegates, to the Situation Room. Our committee, the UN Convention Against Transnational Organized Crime, typically handles global threats. Today, we face a unique challenge: the case of a missing Honda Civic. This dashboard is your tool to analyze the facts, understand the stakeholders, and navigate the complex (and absurd) legal landscape before us.

Our central task is to determine if this is a simple classroom dispute or a case falling under the **Palermo Protocol**. To have jurisdiction, we must find evidence of:

  • An **Organized Criminal Group** (3+ persons).
  • A **Serious Crime** (punishable by 4+ years).
  • A **Transnational Nature** (crossing borders).

IV. Proposed Resolution Paths

The debate will likely consolidate around three potential outcomes. Review these paths to understand the end-game for this committee and identify your potential allies and opposition.

1. The Interventionist Path

Invoke Article 3 of the Palermo Protocol. Mandate a full, Interpol-led international retrieval operation for the Honda Civic, citing high-value organized crime.

FAVORED BY:

Apalı, Beypınar, GMCCA

2. The Dismissal Path

Explicitly state that UNCATOC has no jurisdiction. Reclassify the incident as a sovereign, domestic educational matter and condemn the waste of UN resources.

FAVORED BY:

Counsel (Teacher), Ministry of Ed., Russian Federation

3. The Fact-Finding Path

Delay a full investigation. Authorize a preliminary, UNODC-led fact-finding mission to report on the credibility of the claims before committing resources. May also recommend mediation.

FAVORED BY:

Interpol, UNODC, Vatican City, Japan (conditional)
